Temi's Big Day!
22 April 2025
The morning

She was awake before anyone else. Fully dressed, shoes on the wrong feet, standing in the hallway at half past six — announcing that she was ready. Ready for what, exactly, she couldn't say. Just ready.

The party

Fourteen children. Two broken decorations before anyone arrived. The bouncy castle slightly smaller than advertised. She didn't notice any of it — she was already running.

The moment

We turned off the lights. Everyone sang. She looked at the six candles for a long, serious moment — like she was deciding something important. Then she blew them all out in one go and screamed.

Growing up

Six already. One year ago she couldn't reach the kitchen counter. Somehow that feels like both yesterday and a long time ago.
