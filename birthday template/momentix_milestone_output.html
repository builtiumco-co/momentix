Birthday · 70 years

Ngozi Adaeze Okafor

"Still she dances."

10 May 2025
Lagos, Nigeria
Looking back

She was born in a village that no longer has its old name. She walked to school before there was a road. She raised five children in three different cities, kept a home wherever she landed, and made each one feel like it had always been there. Seventy years reads like a quiet miracle when you know where it started.

What she built

Not buildings. Not titles. She built people. Every one of her children carries something of her — the way she listens without interrupting, the way she prays as though she expects an answer, the way she laughs from somewhere deep and unguarded.

The celebration

The compound had not seen this many people since her husband's funeral, fifteen years ago. But this time the sound was different — louder, lighter, unafraid. They danced for two hours. She outlasted most of them.

The legacy

Her grandchildren do not yet understand what they are inheriting. They will. The stories she tells, the way she holds her ground, the warmth she extends to strangers — these things travel forward.

Seventy years, and the best of her is still arriving.
