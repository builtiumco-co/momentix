Nne's 70th Birthday
10 May 2025 · Lagos
The Gathering

The compound was alive before she arrived. Folded chairs crowded every corner, garlands of yellow and white wound around the gate, and the smell of jollof smoke carried from the kitchen across the road. Her children had been there since dawn, whispering and laughing and pretending not to be nervous. By the time the car turned onto the street, eighty people were holding their breath.

The Moment

Nobody had warned her. When the gate swung open and she saw the faces — her sisters, her grandchildren, old friends she hadn't seen in years — she stopped walking and put both hands over her mouth. There was a beat of pure silence before the ululation started. Her youngest daughter ran to her first, and then everyone did.

"Seventy years and she still makes every room feel like home."

The People

Her children came from three states. Her church women wore matching lace — deep burgundy, chosen months in advance. The grandchildren sat in a row at the front, freshly pressed, trying to behave. Her eldest brother gave a blessing so long and beautiful that by the end of it, no one was dry-eyed.

Looking Ahead

Seventy years of grace, and still she dances. Still she prays over everyone who enters her door. If there is a secret to a life this full, she has never said it plainly — but today, watching her move through the crowd, it felt obvious.
